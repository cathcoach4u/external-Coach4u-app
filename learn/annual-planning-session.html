🗺️ Annual Planning Session

Two days, once a year, to refresh the entire One-Page Plan and set the year ahead.

What this is

A two-day off-site session, once a year, where the leadership team refreshes the entire One-Page Plan and sets the year ahead. The keystone of the operating system — everything else hangs off it.

Why it matters

The world changes more in 12 months than you think. An annual reset keeps your strategy from quietly drifting into irrelevance. The off-site gives you the physical distance to think strategically — sitting in your own boardroom never produces the same quality of thinking.

Pre-work (1–2 weeks before)

  • Every leader completes the Team Check-in (17 statements, 1–5 scores)
  • Aggregate results circulated 48 hours before Day 1 — each leader reads them
  • Each leader reviews last year’s annual session notes + their One-Thing personal commitment
  • Book the venue: off-property, no daily commute, dinner together Day 1 night
  • Bring: notebook, current numbers, calendar, an open mind

Day 1 — Where are we?

  1. Check-in — 30 min. Bests, what’s working / not, expectations for the two days. Capture “not working” items as issues.
  2. Review the prior year — 45 min.
    • Walk last year’s 1-Year Plan goal-by-goal
    • Grade the year (A / B / C / D / F) — honestly
    • 30 min on lessons: what would we do differently?
  3. Team Health — 60 min. Patrick Lencioni’s Five Dysfunctions of a Team pyramid. Rate each rung 1–10:
    • Trust (foundation — vulnerability-based)
    • Productive conflict (the team can disagree openly)
    • Commitment (everyone leaves the room aligned)
    • Accountability (peers hold each other to it)
    • Results (the collective result over individual ones)
    The lowest-rated rung is where Day 1 needs to spend time.
  4. One Thing exercise — 60 min. (Every annual.) Each leader sits in the “hot seat” while the others share:
    • One thing this person does that they admire most
    • One thing this person should start / stop / continue doing for the good of the team
    The person in the hot seat then commits to one personal change they’ll work on this year. The commitment carries into every quarterly session as a check-in point. This is the highest-leverage 60 minutes of the two days — don’t skip it.
  5. Operating-system health check — 45 min. Rate each component 1–10 (same list as quarterly — strategy / accountability / meetings / numbers / issues / process). Below-7 items become issues.
  6. SWOT — 60 min. Strengths / Weaknesses / Opportunities / Threats. Each quadrant becomes a list of issues.
  7. One-Page Plan review — 90 min.
    • Walk section by section
    • Challenge every line — “is this still true?”
    • Update Core Values, Core Focus, Marketing Strategy, Leadership Team as needed

Day 1 evening: team dinner. Off-property. Real conversations happen here — don’t skip it.

Day 2 — Where are we going?

  1. 3-Year Outlook refresh — 60 min. Rebuild from scratch or update. Run the Targets exercise. Close-your-eyes test at the end — does everyone see it?
  2. Issues list & IDS — 90 min. Work through the top issues from Day 1 using the 3-step protocol. Don’t leave the room with anything unresolved that can be resolved here.
  3. 1-Year Plan — 60 min. Revenue, profit, 3–7 measurable goals. Each with an owner. Closing test: “If we hit this, will it be a good year?”
  4. Q1 goals — 30 min. From the 1-Year Plan, pick the 3–7 priorities for the first quarter. Run the Quarterly Goals exercise.
  5. Cascade plan — 30 min. Who tells whom? When? How? The team can’t execute what they don’t know about. Don’t leave without it written down.
  6. Conclude — 15 min. Each leader rates the session 1–10 with one sentence why. Lock the date for next year’s annual.

Common pitfalls

  • Running it in the office. Get offsite — physical removal helps strategic thinking.
  • Inviting too many people. Leadership team only (4–8 people). Bigger groups produce vaguer plans.
  • Skipping the One Thing exercise because it feels touchy-feely. It’s the highest-leverage hour of the two days.
  • Treating Day 1 as warmup. Half the decisions get made on Day 1.
  • Not pre-circulating the Team Check-in results. You’ll waste 90 min mining what the data already shows.
  • Skipping the team dinner. The strategic conversations that don’t happen in the meeting room happen there.
Coach’s tip
Schedule next year’s annual session before this one ends. If you wait, it’ll slip into a busy quarter, then slip again. The annual is the keystone of the year — protect the date like the most important board meeting you have, because it is.
