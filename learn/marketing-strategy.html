📣 Marketing Strategy

Who you serve, what makes you different, the process clients walk through, and the guarantee that backs it.

What this is

Four short statements that together define how you go to market: Target Market, 3 Differentiators, Our Process, and Our Guarantee. Each must align with your Core Focus — if it doesn’t, the strategy fights itself.

Why it matters

Without a clear position, every customer takes a slightly different journey, every salesperson tells a slightly different story, and conversion rate suffers. The four statements aren’t marketing copy — they’re a decision filter. Once you have them, every lead, pitch, and proposal passes through.

The Rule of 7: a customer or prospect needs to hear your message at least 7 times before they hear it for the first time. The four statements are what you say, consistently, across every channel.

Exercise 1 — Target Market (“The List”)

Time: ~20 minutes. The goal is a customer profile so specific you could pass the whole world through it as a filter and produce a finite list with names and contact info.

  1. 5 quiet minutes. Each leader silently brainstorms the ideal customer across three lenses:
    • Demographic — who they are. Type of business, size, revenue band, industry.
    • Geographic — where they are. City, state, country, region.
    • Psychographic — how they think. What they value, what they fear, what stage they’re in.
  2. Capture on the board, 10 min. One lens at a time — demographic first, then geographic, then psychographic. Write every contribution; refine later.
  3. Refine to a single profile, 5 min. Walk top to bottom. Add connecting phrases so the description reads as one customer, not three lists. Aim for 80% agreement, not perfect.

The test: can you produce The List — a finite roster of named prospects who match the profile? If you can name 50 specific businesses (or 100 named people), the profile’s sharp enough.

Exercise 2 — 3 Differentiators

Time: ~20 minutes. The 3 things that make you different and better in the mind of your target market — not a list of features.

  1. 5 quiet minutes. Each leader writes down every possible differentiator. Don’t filter yet.
  2. Share around the table, 5 min. Write every answer on the board.
  3. Keep / Kill / Combine, 10 min. Get to 3 (occasionally 4).

The competitor test: line yourself up against every competitor. They may do one. Some may do two. You are the only one that does all three. The combination is what makes you unique — not any single one.

Once landed, the three differentiators become the common thread running through every sales conversation, every marketing asset, every proposal. (If you try to please everyone, you’ll be remembered by no-one. Southwest Airlines is famous for 3LFs — Low Fares, Lots of Flights, Lots of Fun. Pick three, own three.)

Exercise 3 — Our Process (YES / NO decision)

Time: ~15 minutes. Decide whether you want one. If yes, build it later.

What it is: a named, 3–7 step process showing the prospect their journey with you — from first meeting through years of follow-up. One page, full colour, illustrated, named (“The X Method” / “Our 4-Step System”), printed and laminated.

Why it works:

  • Gives the prospect peace of mind — they can see what working with you looks like
  • Differentiates you — most competitors don’t have one
  • Creates sales-conversation consistency — everyone tells the same story

Decide today — YES or NO. If YES, leave on the One-Page Plan and make it a future Quarterly Goal to actually build it. If NO, remove it from the plan; no half-measures.

Exercise 4 — Our Guarantee (YES / NO decision)

Time: ~10 minutes. Same shape as Process — decide YES or NO.

What it is: a promise that makes it easier for the prospect to say yes. Takes their biggest fear off the table. Ideally backed up with teeth — not just a vague reassurance.

Forms it can take:

  • Money-back if a defined outcome doesn’t happen by a defined date
  • Service-level guarantee (“We respond within X”, “Seen within 29 minutes”)
  • A behavioural pledge / promise / commitment — weaker words, but they still work if backed by behaviour

~50% of leadership teams say yes, ~50% say no. Either is fine — what matters is the decision. Decide today.

Common pitfalls

  • Target Market that says “everyone”. If The List has more than ~500 entries, the profile is too wide.
  • Adjective differentiators (“trusted”, “professional”, “friendly”). They’re commodities — every competitor says them.
  • A differentiator that competitors could honestly claim too. Sharpen until you’re the only business that does all three.
  • A Process described as your sales funnel (“Discovery → Proposal → Close”). The client doesn’t care about your funnel — name their journey.
  • A toothless guarantee. “We promise we’ll try our best” helps no-one decide.
  • Letting the strategy live in a doc no-one reads. Print it. Train every salesperson on the same words.
Coach’s tip
Once defined, audit every customer touch-point: website, proposals, sales scripts, voicemail, the way you answer the phone. Every key page should reflect the position. If your homepage and your About page tell different stories, prospects don’t trust either — the inconsistency reads as confusion.
