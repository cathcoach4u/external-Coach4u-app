⚡ Issues

Capture problems the moment they surface. Discuss and resolve in the weekly meeting.

What this is

The running list of problems, bottlenecks, and risks blocking the team. Worked through in the weekly meeting using a 3-step protocol — Identify, Discuss, Resolve.

Why it matters

Issues left unspoken become resentments, missed deadlines, and surprise resignations. Issues captured in writing become decisions. The team that aggressively works its issues list will out-execute the team that doesn’t, even with worse strategy.

The 3-step protocol

Every issue gets walked through these three steps, in order:

  1. Identify. Most issues are stated badly — the stated problem is rarely the real issue. Dig down.
    • “Our developer’s slow” → real issue: spec quality? prioritization? capacity? hiring need? wrong tools?
    • “Sales is off” → real issue: lead volume? close rate? sales rep? product-market fit?
    Spend 60 seconds confirming the real issue before discussing.
  2. Discuss. Get everything on the table — but say it only once. Repeating yourself is politicking, not contributing. Once everyone’s said what they need to, move to solve. Most discussions take 3–5 minutes, not 30.
  3. Resolve. Agree on a plan that makes the issue go away forever (or as close as possible).
    • It’s more important that you decide than what you decide. A “wrong” decision moves the team forward; no decision blocks five other things.
    • Listen for the sound of agreement — when the team lands, you’ll hear it.
    • The owner of the action goes on the todo list with a by-when date.

The 80% rule + “disagree and commit”

8 out of 10 times, the team will reach agreement naturally. The other 2 times, the most senior leader (CEO / Integrator / owner) decides. Then everyone commits — even those who disagreed.

This is disagree and commit. The alternative is paralysis. A decided issue is done; if it turns out to be wrong, it comes back as a new issue — with new data.

Prioritize in 30 seconds

At the start of issues time, the facilitator picks the top 3 — gut feel, not a vote, not a debate. Laser-focus on those three. Everything else waits.

If you only get through 2 in the meeting, that’s fine. Resist the urge to skim through 15.

How to work the list every week

  1. Bring the prioritized list to the meeting
  2. Take #1: Identify (60 seconds) → Discuss (3–5 min) → Resolve (action + owner + date)
  3. Move to #2, then #3
  4. Anything taking more than ~10 min is probably stuck on Identify — back up and dig

Common pitfalls

  • Issues that are really todos. If it just needs doing, it’s a todo. Issues need discussion.
  • Hesitating to raise an issue because it feels personal. The team needs the friction more than it needs comfort.
  • Spending the meeting on issue #1 and never reaching #2–3. Time-box.
  • Endless discussion. After 10 min on one issue, force a decision — even an imperfect one.
  • Re-litigating decided issues. Once decided, it’s done. Disagree and commit.
Coach’s tip
A short, well-worked issues list is a sign of a healthy team. A list that grows by 5 every week and never shrinks is a sign of avoidance. If the list is over 30, you’re not working it — you’re storing it. Schedule a one-off 2-hour clear-the-decks session and IDS through 10 of them in a row.
