⭐ Step 1 — Name Your Values

List the 3–5 non-negotiable beliefs that guide how your company operates — the things you'd hire and fire for.

Tip: Fewer is better. 3–5 strong values beats a long list nobody remembers. Think about what would make you fire someone even if they were performing well.

✎ Step 2 — Define Each Value

Write what each value actually means in your company — in your own words, not a dictionary definition.

Tip: A great definition is 1–2 sentences a new team member would immediately understand. Avoid generic words like "excellence" without explanation.
Step 2 of 3

◎ Step 3 — Values in Action

For each value, describe the specific behaviours you'd expect to see. This makes values observable and real.

Tip: Think about your first day explaining this to a new team member. What would you point to? What would they see someone doing?
