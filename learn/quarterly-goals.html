🏆 Quarterly Goals

3–7 priorities for the quarter, with an owner and a clear "done".

What this is

3–7 priorities for the quarter, with an owner and a clear “done” definition. Reviewed weekly. The big rocks that, if completed, move the business forward toward the 1-Year Plan.

Why it matters

Quarters are the natural unit of business momentum — short enough to be urgent, long enough to ship something real. Without quarterly priorities, you’ll work hard for 90 days and not be sure what you achieved.

Two metaphors worth knowing:

  • Big rocks first (Stephen Covey). If you have a jar to fill with rocks, pebbles, sand, and water — put the rocks in first. Everything else fills in around them. Try the other order and the rocks won’t fit.
  • Laser, not lightbulb (Al Reis). A lightbulb lights a room. A laser of the same wattage cuts through steel. Focus is the multiplier.

The exercise (~75 mins)

  1. Silent listing, 10 min. Each leader silently writes their 3–7 candidate priorities for the quarter. No talking. Quiet thinking only.
  2. Write on the board, 10 min. Around the table, each person reads one priority and writes it on the board. Then the next person, then the next. Keep going until everyone’s exhausted their list.
  3. Keep / Kill / Combine — colour-coded, 20 min. Three colours:
    • Green — Keep, this is a rock
    • Red — Kill, not a quarterly priority
    • Yellow — Combine, same idea as another
    Vote item by item. Majority rules. DO NOT erase the killed list — it becomes the menu for individual goals later.
  4. Star round, 10 min. Each leader gets 3 stars. Place them next to the top 3 they think the company should prioritize. Tally. The top-voted are the company rocks.
  5. SMART filter, 10 min. For each surviving rock: Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, Time-bound. If it fails the test, rewrite or drop.
  6. First step + by-when, 10 min. For each rock, the owner names the first concrete step they’ll take this week and the date they’ll have it done by. Without a first step, the rock won’t start.
  7. Closing read-back, 5 min. Read all rocks aloud. Then ask: “On [date 90 days from now], when we’ve done all of these, will this have been a good 90 days?” If no, something’s missing — add it now.

Company rocks first, then individual. Once the company list is set, each leader picks 1–3 personal rocks from the killed list or their own backlog — their personal commitments this quarter.

Common pitfalls

  • “Aspirational” goals nobody knows how to deliver. If the owner can’t name the first 3 steps, the goal isn’t ready.
  • “Company goals” with no owner. Someone has to be on the hook.
  • More than 7 rocks. The focus is gone — cut.
  • Setting them and not reviewing weekly. The weekly status check is what makes the system work.
  • Erasing the killed list. Those ideas become this quarter’s individual goals or next quarter’s rocks.
Coach’s tip
At week 6 of the quarter, every off-track rock needs a hard conversation. Add resources, cut scope, or accept that you won’t ship it. Don’t drift — the cost of drift is week 12 finding out you missed three.
