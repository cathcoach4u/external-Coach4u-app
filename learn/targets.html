🎯 Targets

Your 10-year vision, 3-year outlook, and 1-year plan — so today's work has a finish line.

What this is

Three time horizons (10 / 3 / 1 years) with concrete revenue, profit and goal numbers. The further out you go, the bigger the vision; the closer in, the more specific the commitments.

Why it matters

"Grow the business" isn't a target — you can't tell if you're winning. Concrete numbers at 10 / 3 / 1 years give every decision a benchmark.

How to do it well

  1. 10-Year Vision: write it as if it's already true. "By 2036 we are…" — team size, client base, geography, what you're known for. Don't worry about plausibility yet; what would make you proud?
  2. 3-Year Outlook: the version of the business that exists 3 years out. Specific revenue, profit, headcount, capabilities. Roughly half-way to the 10-year vision.
  3. 1-Year Plan: the 3–7 things that, if done this year, get you to the 3-year outlook. Each has a measurable result and an owner.

Common pitfalls

  • Vague numbers ("more"). If it's not a dollar amount or a count, it won't drive behaviour.
  • A 1-Year Plan with 30 items. Pick 3–7. Everything else is noise.
Coach’s tip
Review the 1-Year Plan every quarter. If a goal is 25% done at the 50% mark, replan — don't just hope.
